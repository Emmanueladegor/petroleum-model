Four Transmission Channels from the Iran War
1. Strait of Hormuz Closure → Oil Price Surge
POSITIVE
The Strait carries ~20% of global oil supply. Its near-complete closure has pushed Brent from $64.85 (budget) to $115+ today — a 77.9% premium over your benchmark. This is the single dominant positive variable for your group, overwhelming both the production shortfall and FX drag.
Impact: +₦T in annual royalties (price only, at budget volumes)
2. Nigeria Cannot Ramp Production → Missed Upside
NEGATIVE (STRUCTURAL)
Nigeria's production fell from 1.63 mbpd (January) to 1.48 mbpd (February) — well below the 1.84 mbpd budget target. Pipeline vandalism, crude theft, and operational inefficiencies mean Nigeria cannot capitalise on the high-price environment. Every 100,000 bpd shortfall at $115 costs approximately ₦595B/year in unrealised royalties.
Impact: −₦T vs what 1.84 mbpd at $115 would yield
3. Naira Strengthening → Conversion Loss on USD Collection
NEGATIVE
As Nigeria earns more USD from high oil prices, CBN foreign reserves have reached $50.45B — the highest in 13 years — strengthening the naira. You collect royalties in USD; at ₦1,380 versus the ₦1,400 budget assumption, every dollar converts to ₦20 less than planned. The drag is modest compared to the price windfall but grows if the naira continues to appreciate.
Impact: −₦T annually (at current production and price)
4. Crude-Backed Financing Obligations → Revenue Timing Risk
CAUTION
A portion of Nigeria's crude proceeds are already pre-committed via crude-for-product swap arrangements and NNPC refinery-linked obligations. Higher prices increase the naira value of these pre-committed barrels but may slow the rate at which the full upside flows into federation royalty receipts — creating a timing lag rather than a permanent loss.
Impact: Timing and scale risk — may delay full revenue capture into Q3/Q4 2026
Net Assessment
Despite the naira strengthening and persistent production shortfall, the Iran war oil price premium is so large (+77.9%) that your group is on track to materially EXCEED the ₦8.47T target at current conditions.

BIGGEST RISK: A rapid Iran war de-escalation driving prices back to $65–75/bbl, combined with the ongoing production shortfall, could push royalties BELOW ₦8.47T target.
